## Recurring themes

### 1. Architecture-sketch / installed-API mismatch resurfaced — at the ergonomics layer
Epic 1's recurring theme was "architecture sketches under-specified library semantics" (every story past 1.1 found one library/spec mismatch). Epic 2's only story repeated the pattern, but at a different layer: the spec's dispatch mechanism (`container.override(StrategiesGroup.branch_prefix_strategy, cc_instance)` — overriding the *resolved* Factory rather than the *bound* kwarg) was technically correct against modern-di's installed API, but it produced a code structure where "the Factory named `branch_prefix_strategy` resolves to a `ConventionalCommitsStrategy` instance." The story Dev Notes explicitly flagged this as "counter-intuitive but correct"; review (D1) treated that acknowledgement as a yellow flag and demanded a new `current_strategy` Factory be introduced as the single use-case binding point. The two named Factories (`branch_prefix_strategy`, `conventional_commits_strategy`) are now both directly resolvable but unused-in-production — pre-positioned for Story 4.8 shadow-mode (which would resolve both at once).

**Lesson:** "counter-intuitive but correct" in spec Dev Notes is a code smell. The 1.7 retro flagged the same pattern around `@modern_di_typer.inject` (abandoned mid-story because the contract didn't fit a per-CLI-call container rebuild). Epic 2 reproduced it. Treat it as a review-priority signal.

### 2. Review pushed the parser toward leniency, not strictness
Three of the five D-patches moved the CC parser toward more permissive parsing of real-world commit messages:
- **D2** — `decide()` now `lstrip()`s each `body_lines()` entry before the `BREAKING CHANGE:` `startswith` check. Indented footers (tab- or space-prefixed) now detect.
- **D3** — `body_lines()` collects post-subject non-blank lines *even when no blank-line separator is present*. The CC v1.0.0 spec requires a blank-line-separated footer; reality on GitLab/GitHub squash bodies often omits it.
- **D5** — `footers()` renamed `body_lines()` to honestly reflect its semantics (the helper doesn't distinguish footers from prose; it returns *all* non-blank lines after the subject).

The story's initial framing was spec-strict (CC v1.0.0 anchored at every parsing decision). Review surfaced that spec-strict here means "rejects real merge commits." The CC strategy now accepts more than CC v1.0.0 calls valid — but it never *rejects* a valid input. Strict-superset hardening.

### 3. Pydantic field-level validation closed a config-reachability gap
**D4** added `_VALID_TYPE_RE = ^[a-z]+$` + a `@pydantic.field_validator("minor_types", "patch_types")` on `ConventionalCommitsConfig`. Without it, a user-configured `minor_types=("FEAT",)` or `("feat-x",)` would silently produce `Bump.NONE` for every commit (the regex `^[a-z]+` in `decide()` would never match). Now those configurations raise `ValidationError` at config-load. Pattern mirrors Story 1.6's `Field(min_length=1)` addition. "Validators-at-config-load" is now an established defensive pattern; future configs default to it.

### 4. Marina test fixture re-narrated mid-implementation
The originally-planned fixture for the cross-strategy integration test was the classic merge-commit shape: `"Merge branch 'feature/foo' into main\n\nfeat!: breaking foo"`. CC strategy parses *only the subject line* (`_commit_parse.subject_line()` returns `"Merge branch 'feature/foo' into main"`), so `_TYPE_PATTERN.match()` failed and CC returned `Bump.NONE` — not the intended `MAJOR`. Re-fixtured to `"feat!: drop python 3.9"` (squash-merge style): branch-prefix sees no `Merge branch` marker and emits `no_merge_commit` (no tag); CC parses the `!` and emits `MAJOR → 2.0.0`. The journey is still demonstrated — same fixture, divergent outcomes — but the divergence is `no_merge_commit` vs `MAJOR`, NOT the original `MINOR (1.5.0)` vs `MAJOR (2.0.0)` framing.

**Knock-on:** PRD wording, mkdocs content (Story 4.4), and the trust-surface narrative (Story 4.6) all need to reflect the actual demonstrated journey, not the pre-implementation framing.

### 5. Refactor under a coverage gate silently shifts behavior
`BranchPrefixStrategy.decide()` swapped `commit.message.split("\n", 1)[0]` for `_commit_parse.subject_line(commit.message)` (AC9 / retro A3). Old semantics: returns the literal first line, including a leading blank if the message starts with `\n`. New semantics: skips leading blanks. The Story 1.6 100% branch gate (`just test-branch-strategies`) held — but only because the existing tests don't exercise leading-blank-line messages. That's the precise input the deferred test gap (item 8 in Story 2.1's deferred-work) flags. The gate gave a false signal of equivalence.

**Lesson:** a 100% branch gate proves all branches execute; it does NOT prove behavior equivalence under refactor. When the underlying operation's semantics change, the gate should be paired with a property-based or input-class regression test. Future refactors of parsing helpers should add an explicit-input regression alongside the gate check.

### 6. Review-cycle discipline matured further
Epic 1 retro tracked the "Patches / Decision / Defer / Dismissed" bucketing as a 1.2 → 1.7 maturation. Epic 2 closed with 5 decisions + 8 patches + 8 deferred + 8 dismissed in one story — and *all 5 decisions converted to patches*, none deferred. The deferred-list is genuinely future-looking (defensive gates, cross-validation, BOM handling) rather than "ran out of time" leftovers. The deferred test gap (item 8) is correctly attributed to a do-not-touch list, not implementer choice.

### 7. The "named factory" indirection now has three slots
After Story 2.1: `StrategiesGroup` exposes `branch_prefix_strategy`, `conventional_commits_strategy`, and `current_strategy`. The first two are directly resolvable but unused in `_use_case.py`; the third is the use-case binding. Production flow only touches `current_strategy`. The other two are pre-positioned for shadow-mode (Story 4.8) where both must be resolvable simultaneously. Cost: two factories sitting unused for the v1.0 release; benefit: shadow-mode wiring is structurally already in place. Future readers of `ioc.py` will need a one-line architecture comment to navigate this. (Logged as B5 below.)

## Struggles / friction points

1. **5 decision-needed findings is high for a single story.** Stories 1.1 (none), 1.2 (one minor pivot), 1.3 (clean), 1.4 (timezone bug), 1.6 (two post-review patches), and 1.7 (3 decisions) all had lower decision-count surfaces. Story 1.5 — the largest Epic 1 story — had 17 patches but they were patches, not architecture-level decisions. Story 2.1's 5 decisions all touched architecture (DI dispatch shape, parser leniency, config validation, helper naming). Review continues to be load-bearing for architectural correctness — *more* load-bearing in Epic 2 than in any Epic 1 story except 1.5.
2. **The `container.override(branch_prefix_strategy, cc_instance)` dispatch was structurally smelly and shipped to review anyway.** The story author flagged the trade-off in Dev Notes ("counter-intuitive but correct") and proceeded. Review rejected it within hours. Lesson: when the spec explicitly defends a counter-intuitive choice, that defense belongs in a separate "alternatives considered" doc *and* a review-priority callout, not buried in implementation notes. Better: prototype both options and pick in review.
3. **A1 was a retro action item that turned out to be discovery, not implementation.** "Decide on `BumpStrategy` Protocol surface extension" sounded like an action item; in execution it was a 5-minute architecture-reread and a one-paragraph Debug Log entry. Future retros should distinguish *decision* items (cheap if the answer is "no") from *implementation* items (always non-zero work). Mixing them inflates the action-item count and dilutes follow-through metrics.
4. **`_commit_parse.body_lines()` is permissive on whitespace-only blank-line detection.** Uses `line.strip()` rather than `line == ""` to identify the subject/body separator. A pathological body like `"feat: x\n   \nBREAKING CHANGE: leaked"` (intentionally indented body line) would false-trigger `MAJOR`. Deferred. The spec said "blank line"; the implementation treats whitespace-only as blank. Real inputs likely never hit this — but the helper is now the canonical commit-message parser for the project, so the looseness has a long shelf life.
5. **The `subject_line()` refactor under the 100% branch gate (theme #5) is a structural risk pattern.** Every future helper-extraction that propagates through both strategies will face the same false-equivalence trap. The 1.6 gate is a regression canary against branch-coverage loss, not against behavioral change. As more shared helpers land (Epic 3's doctor checks may want a shared cause-fragment matcher; Epic 4.8's shadow-mode will want both strategies' parsers running in parallel), the gate will need to be supplemented.

## Tech debt added by Epic 2

- **`StrategiesGroup` now has three Factories, two of them production-dead for v1.0.** `branch_prefix_strategy` and `conventional_commits_strategy` are resolvable but unused in `_use_case.py` (which binds to `current_strategy`). They exist for Story 4.8 shadow-mode. Cost: two factories sitting unused; benefit: shadow-mode wiring is structurally ready. Mitigation: a one-line architecture comment in `ioc.py` would prevent future readers from "cleaning up" the unused Factories. (B5 below.)
- **`body_lines()` over-collects, by design.** The helper returns *all* non-blank lines after the subject (renamed from `footers()` in D5 because the original name lied). The current consumer (CC strategy's `BREAKING CHANGE:` scan) tolerates over-collection — the prefix-check filters out body prose. A future consumer wanting actual CC footers (`Refs:`, `Co-Authored-By:`, `Closes #N`) will need a stricter parser. The honest name flags this; no production cost today.
- **`subject_line()` quietly changed `BranchPrefixStrategy` behavior for leading-blank-line messages.** Pre-refactor: returned literal first line including leading blank. Post-refactor: skips leading blanks. Tests don't exercise the input class (deferred). Branch-prefix consumers in the wild that authored a leading-blank merge-commit subject now get a different first-line. The 100% branch gate held without catching the divergence.
- **`current_strategy` Factory's dispatch is `if/elif` over `settings.strategy: Literal[…]`.** Adding a third strategy to the Literal *without updating dispatch* silently falls through to branch-prefix. Pydantic `Literal` validation catches typos but won't catch newly-added-and-undispatched strategies. (B3 below addresses the defensive `else`.)

## Forward-looking concerns for Epic 3

*(Epic 3 has since landed — Stories 3.1 and 3.2 are both `done` as of 2026-05-29. Concerns preserved as historical record; outcomes annotated.)*

**C1 — Exit-code dominance vs `__main__.py` exit-code path.**
Story 1.7 established `__main__.py` as the single exit-code mapper. Story 3.1 (doctor chain runner + exit-code dominance) introduces a parallel exit-code path that must compose without contradiction. Risk: two sources of truth.
**Outcome:** Story 3.1 chose a `_DOMINANCE` table + `_exit_code_for_failed_check` mapping cause-fragments to error codes. Works for the current `GitLabProvider` cause vocabulary but accepts a `StopIteration` risk if a new `SemvertagError` subclass appears without `_DOMINANCE` extension — documented in `_bmad/deferred-work.md §3-1` items 1–4.

**C2 — `_commit_parse.body_lines()` over-collection bites doctor checks.**
A `commit-has-no-CC-footer` doctor check would consume `body_lines()` and mis-report indented body prose as a footer.
**Outcome:** Epic 3's doctor checks (3.1 chain runner + 3.2 typer wrapper) don't inspect commit messages — they only probe token/scopes/project access/protected-tags. Helper-leniency surface didn't manifest. Concern survives for future doctor checks.

**C3 — `current_strategy` dispatch lacks defensive `else: raise`.**
Adding a third strategy to `Settings.strategy: Literal[…]` without updating `build_container`'s `if settings.strategy == "conventional-commits":` block silently falls through to branch-prefix.
**Status:** Open as of 2026-05-29. Relevant for v1.x planning. Tracked as B3 below.
